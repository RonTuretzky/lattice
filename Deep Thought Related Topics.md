# 20 Topics Branching from Cockaigne

Threads to explore from the Land of Cockaigne — medieval peasant utopia, food, protest, and the dreams of ordinary people.

---

## Medieval Life & Society

1. **The Great Famine of 1315–1317** — The catastrophe that killed millions and reshaped European society before the Black Death even arrived
2. **Carnival and the World Turned Upside Down** — The medieval tradition of sanctioned chaos, where servants became masters and bishops were mocked for a day
3. **The Medieval Commons** — How peasants actually organized, shared resources, and resisted enclosure long before modern politics

## Utopian Thought

4. **Thomas More's *Utopia* (1516)** — The book that gave us the word, written as a joke that nobody has agreed on for 500 years
5. **The Garden of Eden as Political Idea** — How paradise myths have been used across cultures to justify or challenge the existing order
6. **Utopia vs. Arcadia** — Two competing Western dreams: the perfected city vs. the return to pastoral simplicity

## Food & Hunger as History

7. **The Columbian Exchange** — How the collision of Old and New World foods after 1492 transformed what humanity eats forever
8. **Bread and Revolution** — From Rome's grain dole to the French Revolution's bread riots — how food scarcity topples governments
9. **The Invention of Fasting** — Why nearly every major religion independently arrived at the idea that *not eating* is holy

## Art & Imagination

10. **Hieronymus Bosch's *Garden of Earthly Delights*** — Bruegel's spiritual predecessor, and one of the most bewildering paintings ever made
11. **The Peasant in Art** — How ordinary people went from invisible to central subjects, from Bruegel through Millet to the social realists
12. **Maps of Imaginary Places** — From Cockaigne to Prester John's kingdom — medieval cartography blurred the line between real and mythical geography

## Protest & Power

13. **The Peasants' Revolt of 1381** — When English peasants marched on London, killed the Archbishop of Canterbury, and almost overthrew the feudal order
14. **Hidden Transcripts** — James C. Scott's theory of how oppressed people resist through folklore, jokes, and coded speech rather than open rebellion
15. **The Diggers and Levellers** — 17th-century English radicals who tried to build Cockaigne for real — communal land, no landlords, no hierarchy

## The Body & the Sacred

16. **The Medieval Body** — How people in the Middle Ages understood their own flesh — humors, sin, medicine, and the strange overlap of all three
17. **Holy Feast and Holy Fast** — Caroline Walker Bynum's work on how medieval women used food (eating it and refusing it) as a form of spiritual and political power

## Deep Threads

18. **Why Do Humans Dream of Golden Ages?** — From Hesiod's five ages to the Aboriginal Dreamtime — the near-universal myth that the past was better
19. **Scarcity and the Origins of Morality** — The argument that most moral codes (sharing, restraint, delayed gratification) are fundamentally responses to not having enough
20. **The Death of Peasant Culture** — How industrialization, literacy, and urbanization erased an entire oral civilization, and what fragments survive
